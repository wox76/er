Mission

EraDrive AI

EraDrive is a scalable, single-solution platform for space safety, performance, and defense. It streamlines space traffic management, on-orbit logistics, and advanced remote sensing.

Our AI-native architecture handles everything from deep learning perception and real-time trajectory optimization to automated collision avoidance, allowing partners to focus on their core mission while building a real-time, global space traffic intelligence network.

Core capabilities

Why Choose Us?

EraDrive's modular autonomy products reduce mission complexity, enabling partners to focus on their core mission.

TRL 9 autonomy with 5 heritage missions (12 satellites) across RPO, formation-flying, and swarming

Standardized software-hardware kits that integrate with any spacecraft bus via common interfaces

Deep learning perception, trajectory optimization, reasoning, and space traffic management built-in

THE PROBLEM

Three Pressing Problems in Spaceflight

Legacy space infrastructure faces critical barriers across security, performance, and traffic management.

01

Space is at War

Legacy technology is not ready to counteract adversarial threats, to provide surprise or offense.

How to ensure autonomous navigation in GPS-denied adversarial environments?

02

Space is Demanding

Outdated, rule-based, micromanaged, human-controlled systems are not able to meet requirements.

How to enable fully autonomous, multi-satellite prox ops and formation flight?

03

Space is Crowded

Outdated, ground-based, fragmented space traffic management systems put orbit safety at risk.

How to autonomously detect, track, and avoid collisions with over 1 million RSOs?

FIRESIDE CHAT JUNE 1, 2026

Sumant Sharma (CEO) presented at Stanford University

Stanford Founders Club invites all students and researchers interested in aerospace entrepreneurship to a fireside chat with Sumant Sharma, Co-Founder & CEO of EraDrive and Stanford AeroAstro alumnus, followed by a networking evening. Sumant will share how research from the Stanford Space Rendezvous Lab spun out into a spacecraft autonomy startup, lessons on building in the space sector, and what technical and commercial opportunities are emerging in on-orbit services.
PRESENTATION MAY 28, 2026

Prof. Simone D’Amico, Co-Founder and CSO of EraDrive, to present at Princeton.

Future multi-spacecraft missions require robust autonomous trajectory optimization capabilities to ensure safe and efficient rendezvous operations. This capability hinges on solving non-convex constrained optimal control problems in real time, however traditional iterative methods such as sequential convex programming impose significant computational challenges.
