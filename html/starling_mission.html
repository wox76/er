Starling Mission

The Starling swarm consists of 4 x 6U propulsive CubeSats in LEO, launched in July 2023. StarFOX (2023-24), StarFOX+ (2025), and FALCON (2026) experiments perform autonomous navigation and control.

Imaging 2 x BCT Nano Star Trackers (12x10° FoV)
Compute Xiphos Q7S ARM processor @ 700MHz
Crosslink (optional) CesiumAstro S-Band SDR

Experiment 01

Autonomous Relative Orbit Determination

Relative orbit determination for a persistent target, without prior knowledge of its orbit. Images are taken every 60 seconds and processed on board.

Left: kinematic algorithm independently tracks new target (green square) in images.
Bottom right: orbit estimate for new target is initialized with <10% range error.
Top right: orbit estimate is refined to meter-level errors (RN) and <1% range error (T).

Experiment 02

Autonomous Absolute Orbit Determination

Absolute orbit determination for a spacecraft swarm, using only inter-satellite bearing angles shared over a crosslink. Images are taken every 60 seconds and processed on board.

Left: relative orbit geometry of involved swarm spacecraft. Black lines indicate crosslink transmissions.
Top: images from onboard star tracker cameras with objects tracked in the field of view.
Bottom: absolute (green) and relative (red/blue) orbit estimates for the swarm. Measurement sharing enables autonomous absolute orbit maintenance without GNSS.

Experiment 03

Autonomous RSO Identification & Tracking

Identification and tracking of transient resident space objects (RSOs) in the field of view. Images are taken every 60 seconds and processed on board.

Left: uplinked Space-Track catalog is used to identify RSOs (white squares) in flight.
Top right: residuals between optical measurements of RSOs and their reference positions available from various ground products. Residuals are primarily <100 m.
Bottom right: total number of identified RSOs (>150 during this experiment).

Experiment 04

Multi-Observer Orbit Initialization

Fast initial orbit determination for unknown targets. Two observers track a common target for ~20 minutes, and measurements are shared between observers over the crosslink. An orbit initialization is computed for the target object.

Left: visualization of measurement geometry and algorithm progression.
Right: reconstructed images from the onboard cameras.

Experiment 05

Autonomous Tip-and-Cue (Persistent)

Tip-and-cue for optical tracking of RSOs. Target state information is used on board to plan attitude changes which retain the target in the FOV. Images are taken every 60 seconds.

Left: a target object of interest is identified, tracked, and centered in the camera image. More than 200 target measurements are collected over 1-day period.
Bottom right: the target’s relative orbit (green) is regularly outside of the camera FOV (grey cone). An attitude plan is computed online to enable consistent target observation.

Experiment 06

Autonomous Tip-and-Cue (Opportunistic)

Tip-and-cue for optical tracking of RSOs. A list of RSOs to observe is provided to the satellite, which autonomously chooses an optimal target at each image epoch. Images are taken every 60 seconds.

Left: target objects of interest are identified, tracked, and centered in the camera image. Measurements of more than 50 specified targets of interest are collected over a 1-day period.
Bottom right: the attitude of the camera FOV (grey cone) is controlled online to ensure the target of interest (green) is successfully imaged in the presence of other RSOs (red), maximizing field of regard.

Experiment 07

Alternative Optical Positioning (LEO)

In-orbit demonstration of alternative PNT in LEO, independent from GPS/GNSS.

Left: Resident Space Objects (RSOs) are detected and identified on board using an RSO catalog, and are leveraged as passive optical beacons to estimate the position of the host satellite in real time.
Bottom right: absolute orbit determination errors with respect to ex-post facto GPS products (black), the formal covariance from the on-board filter (gray), and the number of tracked RSO (red). Mean positioning errors are less than 100 m.

Experiment 08

Alternative Optical Positioning (GEO)

Simulation demonstration of alternative PNT in GEO, independent from GPS/GNSS.

Left: Resident Space Objects (RSOs) are detected and identified on board using an RSO catalog, and are leveraged as passive optical beacons to estimate the position of the host satellite in real time.
Bottom right: absolute orbit determination errors with respect to ex-post facto GPS products (black), the formal covariance from the on-board filter (gray), and the number of tracked RSO (red). Mean positioning errors are less than 100 m.

Experiment 09

Proliferated Space Domain Awareness

High-fidelity simulation of global space traffic monitoring (STM) performance via data aggregation from 400 EraDrive modules in orbit, distributed across existing satellite orbits.

Left: simulated views of the LEO RSO population for four specific observers.
Top right: positions of RSOs (red), EraDrive modules (green) and specific observers (cyan).
Bottom right: number of measurements and RSO observations across the constellation.
